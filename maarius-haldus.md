## What He Does
Maarius sits between the field managers and the board. He aggregates procurement needs from multiple departments, reviews budget allocations, and ensures procurement activities align with the organisation's strategic priorities. He's the one who identified the need for an AI tool and initiated the design partnership with Tendly.

## His Key Insight (from meeting)
"The biggest gap is market intelligence — we know our internal information well, but we don't know what's happening on the market. This is the big minus." He specifically wants: fair market pricing, how others solved similar procurements, and what bottlenecks others encountered.

## Pain Points
1. **Market intelligence gap** — no visibility into what comparable organisations pay for similar procurements
2. **CoPilot limitations** — tried it, found search "weaker than Google", document analysis shallow ("reads the first page to save tokens"), and hallucination issues
3. **Knowledge fragmentation** — procurement knowledge lives in individual people's heads, not in a system. When someone leaves, knowledge goes with them.
4. **Process overhead** — too many manual steps between identifying a need and having a published tender

## How He Would Use Tendly Buyer
- **Market intelligence dashboard** — what are others procuring in the social welfare sector, what are pricing trends
- **Cross-department procurement coordination** — see what's being procured across all 69 service units to find consolidation opportunities
- **AI chat for strategic questions** — "Should we do a framework contract or individual procurements for cleaning across 15 units?"
- **Evaluate the product itself** — as design partner, he'll systematically test features and provide feedback

## Test Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Sector Benchmarking
Maarius wants to understand how Hoolekandeteenused's procurement spending compares to similar state organisations.
- **Goal**: Find procurement data for social welfare/care sector across Estonia
- **Expected behaviour**: Sophisticated query, expects data-driven answer with specific numbers
- **Success criteria**: Returns relevant tenders from similar organisations (other care providers, social services agencies), shows spending patterns
- **Failure indicators**: Only shows random tenders, can't filter by sector/organisation type

### Scenario 2: Consolidation Opportunity Detection
Multiple service units are separately procuring cleaning services. Maarius suspects a single framework contract would be cheaper.
- **Goal**: Find data to support (or disprove) the consolidation business case
- **Expected behaviour**: Asks the AI to compare individual vs framework contract approaches, with price data
- **Success criteria**: AI provides relevant examples of both approaches with pricing, suggests framework contract structure, identifies potential savings
- **Failure indicators**: Can't compare approaches, gives theoretical advice without data backing

### Scenario 3: Design Partner Evaluation
Maarius is evaluating whether the Tendly Buyer portal is ready for wider rollout at Hoolekandeteenused.
- **Goal**: Systematically test core features and identify gaps
- **Expected behaviour**: Tries various query types, tests edge cases, checks if results are accurate against data he knows
- **Success criteria**: System handles diverse queries, provides accurate data, works smoothly in Estonian
- **Failure indicators**: Frequent errors, hallucinations, missing data, English-only features, slow performance
