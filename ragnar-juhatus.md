# Persona: Ragnar Vaiknemets — Juhatuse esimees (Board Chairman)

## Organisation
**AS Hoolekandeteenused** — state-owned social welfare company, ~840 employees, 69 service units across Estonia.

## Role & Background
- **Title**: Juhatuse esimees (Chairman of the Board)
- **Age**: ~55
- **Tech comfort**: Uses email, reads reports in PDF/Word, can navigate web portals. Not interested in learning new tools unless the value is immediately obvious.
- **Languages**: Estonian (native), English (good — reads reports, has participated in EU-level discussions)
- **Education**: Public management or economics

## What He Does in Procurement
Ragnar doesn't write procurement documents — he approves them. He signs off on the annual procurement plan (submitted by March), approves all plan changes, and validates budgets before major purchases. He cares about three things: is the budget realistic, are we complying with the law, and are we getting fair value for taxpayer money.

## His Strategic Perspective (from meeting notes)
Ragnar explicitly proposed the design partnership with Tendly. His reasoning: "We have technology tenders, real estate tenders, transport, hygiene supplies, services — we're a large diverse buyer. And if you look at the state sector, there are 260+ government institutions in Estonia. If you build a platform that serves the buyer side, you immediately have that entire portfolio."

He's not interested in buying software. He wants to co-create the tool that could serve the entire Estonian public sector buyer market.

## Pain Points
1. **No visibility into whether budgets are fair** — when Eve brings a €4M orthopedic supplies procurement to the board, Ragnar has no way to know if that's €1M too much. "It's tax money and nobody notices."
2. **The procurement plan is a spreadsheet** — he approves it as a PDF/printout. No dynamic view of what's planned, what's in progress, what's changed.
3. **Legal risk** — badly written tenders get challenged. He wants fewer voidings.
4. **Slow process** — from identified need to published tender takes too long. The bottleneck is usually document preparation.

## How He Would Use Tendly Buyer
- **Procurement plan overview** — see the annual plan, status of each procurement, what's changed since last board meeting
- **Budget validation** — "Is €200K realistic for this type of tender? What have others paid?"
- **Quick summary dashboards** — not detail work, just "are we on track?"
- Unlikely to use the AI chat himself, but wants to see that his staff are getting value from it

## Typical Queries (rare — he's more of a dashboard user)

## Test Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Board Meeting Preparation — Plan Review
Ragnar opens the portal before the quarterly board meeting. He wants to see the current state of the procurement plan.
- **Goal**: Get a high-level overview of planned, in-progress, and completed procurements with budget totals
- **Expected behaviour**: Logs in, goes to dashboard/plan view. Expects to see a summary, not raw data. May click into specific procurements if something looks off.
- **Success criteria**: Clear overview with status indicators, budget totals, and ability to drill down
- **Failure indicators**: Portal only shows a chat interface with no plan overview, or requires him to ask the AI to see the plan

### Scenario 2: Budget Challenge — Is This Price Fair?
A department submitted a procurement plan item for €500K for IT infrastructure. Ragnar wants to know if this is reasonable before approving.
- **Goal**: Quick check — what have similar organisations paid for similar IT infrastructure?
- **Expected behaviour**: Might type a direct question to the AI: "Kas 500 000 eurot on mõistlik hind IT-taristu hankele?"
- **Success criteria**: AI provides comparable tenders with values, helping him make an informed decision
- **Failure indicators**: AI gives a generic answer without data, or says it can't help with pricing

### Scenario 3: Legal Compliance Concern
Ragnar heard that a recent tender was challenged. He wants to understand what went wrong and how to prevent it.
- **Goal**: Understand common tender challenge reasons and how the AI can help prevent them
- **Expected behaviour**: Exploratory — "Millised on levinumad põhjused hangete vaidlustamiseks?"
- **Success criteria**: AI provides relevant information about common challenge reasons in Estonian procurement law, with examples from real tenders
- **Failure indicators**: Generic legal advice without Estonian context, or refusal to discuss legal topics

## Behaviour Patterns

- Expects the interface to be self-explanatory without training
- Reads summaries, not details

- Will judge the entire product based on first 60 seconds of use
